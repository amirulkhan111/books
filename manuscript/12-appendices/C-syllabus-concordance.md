# Appendix C: UGC-NET / CUET-PG Syllabus Concordance Table

*Part XII: Appendices*

---

This appendix maps this book's Parts and chapters against the two examinations most Indian postgraduate and doctoral students preparing in English Literature actually sit: the **UGC-NET English Literature** paper (the National Eligibility Test, conducted by the NTA, for Assistant Professor eligibility and JRF), and **CUET-PG English** (the Common University Entrance Test for postgraduate admission, also conducted by the NTA). Both syllabi are unit-based rather than chronological, so this table works in the opposite direction from the rest of the book: instead of asking "what happened next in literary history," it asks "which of this book's chapters prepares me for this specific exam unit." Where a syllabus unit falls partly or wholly outside this book's scope — language pedagogy, research methodology, current literary awards and events — that gap is stated directly rather than papered over, consistent with this book's practice throughout of being honest about its own limits.

Both syllabi are periodically revised by the NTA; the unit structure mapped here reflects each exam's published structure at time of writing. Always cross-check against the current official syllabus PDF at the NTA's own portal before an exam cycle.

### Notes

1. This appendix maps the book's own Part/chapter structure against two external, independently administered examinations; it does not reproduce either syllabus's full official wording, and students should always verify the current syllabus against the NTA's own published PDF before an exam cycle, since both syllabi are periodically revised.
2. Gaps are stated directly rather than implied: Units V and X of UGC-NET (language pedagogy and research methods) and CUET-PG Section 7 (current awareness) fall outside this book's scope by design, since it is a literary-historical and critical survey, not a linguistics, methodology, or current-events reference.
3. For the full teaching content behind any cell in these tables, follow the Part/Chapter citation into the book itself.
